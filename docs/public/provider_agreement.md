# 1. Introduction

This Provider Agreement ("Agreement") governs participation as a Provider on the UniGPU platform.

By registering hardware, installing the Provider Agent, or accepting Compute Jobs through the Services, you agree to comply with this Agreement, the Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Acceptable Use Policy, and all other applicable UniGPU policies.

If you do not agree to this Agreement, you must not participate as a Provider.

---

# 2. Definitions

Capitalized terms used in this Agreement have the meanings assigned to them in the **Legal Definitions** document unless expressly defined otherwise.

---

# 3. Becoming a Provider

To become a Provider, you must:

- Create a valid UniGPU Account.
- Complete any required identity verification.
- Register accurate hardware information.
- Install and maintain the official UniGPU Provider Agent.
- Accept this Agreement.

UniGPU reserves the right to approve or reject any Provider application at its sole discretion.

---

# 4. Provider Eligibility

Providers represent and warrant that:

- They legally own or are authorized to operate the registered hardware.
- They have the legal authority to provide compute resources.
- Their participation complies with applicable laws.
- Their hardware information is accurate.
- They are not subject to sanctions or restrictions that prohibit participation.

---

# 5. Hardware Requirements

Providers must ensure that listed hardware:

- Matches the specifications displayed on the Marketplace.
- Is capable of executing assigned Jobs.
- Uses supported operating systems and drivers where required.
- Meets UniGPU's minimum software compatibility requirements.
- Remains in good working condition.

Providers must promptly update hardware specifications whenever they change.

Providing false or misleading hardware information is prohibited.

---

# 6. Provider Responsibilities

Providers agree to:

- Maintain secure systems.
- Keep the Provider Agent up to date.
- Execute assigned Jobs faithfully.
- Protect Client confidentiality.
- Maintain accurate Marketplace listings.
- Respond to platform notifications when required.
- Cooperate during fraud investigations.
- Follow UniGPU documentation and operational requirements.

Providers remain responsible for the security of their own infrastructure.

---

# 7. Job Execution

When accepting Compute Jobs, Providers agree to:

- Execute Jobs only through the official Provider Agent.
- Avoid modifying Client workloads.
- Avoid interfering with Job execution.
- Return Job results without unauthorized alteration.
- Allow Jobs to complete unless interruption is necessary for safety, security, or hardware integrity.

Providers may not:

- Read Client files except as technically necessary for execution.
- Copy Client User Content.
- Retain Client files after Job completion.
- Share Client data with third parties.
- Reverse engineer Client software.
- Attempt to identify confidential information contained within workloads.

---

# 8. Security Requirements

Providers are expected to maintain reasonable security measures, including:

- Updated operating systems.
- Supported GPU drivers.
- Firewall protection.
- Strong authentication.
- Secure network configuration.
- Malware protection.
- Physical security where applicable.

Providers must promptly report suspected compromises affecting their systems.

UniGPU may temporarily suspend Providers whose systems are reasonably believed to pose a security risk.

---

# 9. Availability & Maintenance

Providers control when their hardware is available for Jobs.

Providers should:

- Keep availability schedules accurate.
- Perform maintenance during offline periods whenever practical.
- Avoid interrupting active Jobs.

Repeated failure to honor accepted Jobs may negatively affect Provider reputation or Marketplace visibility.

UniGPU does not require Providers to maintain any minimum uptime unless specified under a separate enterprise agreement.

---

# 10. Payments & Payouts

Providers are eligible to receive payouts for successfully completed Jobs in accordance with UniGPU's pricing and payout policies.

Payouts may be adjusted or delayed in cases involving:

- Fraud investigations.
- Billing disputes.
- Chargebacks.
- Platform abuse.
- Violations of UniGPU policies.
- Legal or regulatory requirements.

Providers are responsible for ensuring that payout information remains accurate.

---

# 11. Taxes

Providers are solely responsible for determining, reporting, and paying any taxes applicable to their earnings.

UniGPU does not provide tax advice.

Where required by applicable law, UniGPU may collect tax-related information or make legally required deductions.

---

# 12. Provider Reputation

UniGPU may maintain a reputation system based on factors including:

- Successful Job completion.
- Reliability.
- Availability.
- Performance.
- Client feedback.
- Security compliance.
- Platform policy compliance.

Reputation scores are intended to improve Marketplace quality and may influence Job allocation.

UniGPU reserves the right to determine how reputation metrics are calculated and displayed.

---

# 13. Fraud Prevention

Providers may not:

- Submit false hardware information.
- Manipulate benchmark results.
- Artificially inflate performance.
- Accept Jobs without intending to execute them.
- Interfere with billing.
- Circumvent scheduling systems.
- Create multiple Accounts to manipulate Marketplace rankings.
- Attempt to receive payment for unperformed work.

UniGPU may investigate suspected fraudulent activity at any time.

---

# 14. Suspension & Delisting

UniGPU may suspend, restrict, or permanently remove a Provider from the Marketplace if the Provider:

- Violates this Agreement.
- Violates UniGPU policies.
- Engages in fraud.
- Threatens platform security.
- Provides false hardware information.
- Demonstrates repeated unreliability.
- Attempts to compromise Client workloads.
- Fails required verification.

Where appropriate, UniGPU may provide advance notice before suspension.

Immediate suspension may occur where necessary to protect the Services or other Users.

---

# 15. Intellectual Property

Providers acquire no ownership rights in Client User Content or Job Outputs.

All intellectual property rights remain with their respective owners.

Providers may not copy, distribute, modify, publish, or otherwise use Client User Content except as strictly necessary to execute assigned Jobs.

---

# 16. Confidentiality

Providers acknowledge that Compute Jobs may contain confidential, proprietary, or commercially sensitive information.

Providers agree to:

- Maintain confidentiality.
- Prevent unauthorized access.
- Use Client information solely for Job execution.
- Avoid disclosing confidential information to third parties.

These obligations survive termination of this Agreement.

- Business interruption.
- Network outages.
- Power failures.
- Third-party service failures.
- Lost opportunities.
- Indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages.

Nothing in this Agreement limits liability where such limitation is prohibited by law.

# 18. Indemnification

Providers agree to indemnify and hold harmless UniGPU, its affiliates, officers, employees, contractors, licensors, successors, and agents against any claims, damages, liabilities, losses, costs, or expenses arising from:

- Violation of this Agreement.
- Violation of applicable law.
- Misrepresentation of hardware.
- Security failures within the Provider's infrastructure.
- Negligent or intentional misconduct.

---

# 19. Termination

Providers may discontinue participation at any time by removing their hardware from the Marketplace and uninstalling the Provider Agent.

Termination does not relieve Providers of obligations arising before termination, including payment disputes, confidentiality obligations, or legal compliance.
