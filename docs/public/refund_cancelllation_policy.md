# 1. Introduction

This Refund & Cancellation Policy ("Policy") explains how refunds, cancellations, wallet balances, Compute Credits, billing disputes, and payment reversals are handled on the UniGPU platform.

By purchasing Services, adding funds to your Wallet, or submitting Compute Jobs, you agree to this Policy.

This Policy should be read together with the Terms of Service, Client Agreement, Privacy Policy, and all other applicable UniGPU policies.

# 3. Scope

This Policy applies to:

- Wallet deposits
- Compute Credits
- Compute Jobs
- Marketplace transactions
- Provider payouts
- Enterprise Services
- Promotional credits
- Billing disputes

# 4. Wallet & Compute Credits

Clients may add funds to their UniGPU Wallet using supported payment methods.

Wallet balances may be used to purchase:

- Compute Jobs
- Storage services
- Premium platform features
- Other Services offered by UniGPU

Unless required by applicable law, Wallet balances are non-transferable and may not be exchanged for cash.

Wallet balances do not accrue interest.

---

# 5. Job Cancellation

Clients may cancel a Job before execution begins.

If a Job has not started:

- No compute charges will apply.
- Reserved funds, if any, will be returned to the Wallet.

Once a Job has begun execution, compute charges may apply based on actual resource consumption.

Cancellation of a running Job does not automatically entitle the Client to a full refund.

---

# 6. Refund Eligibility

Refunds may be granted in circumstances including:

- Platform-side scheduling failures.
- Duplicate billing.
- Payment processing errors.
- Jobs that never started due to UniGPU platform failures.
- Incorrect billing caused by verified system errors.
- Unauthorized transactions confirmed after investigation.

Refund requests are evaluated individually.

Approval of one refund request does not guarantee approval of future requests.

---

# 7. Automatic Refunds

UniGPU may automatically issue refunds or Wallet credits where:

- A Provider fails to start an assigned Job.
- A Job cannot be scheduled due to platform failure.
- Duplicate charges are detected.
- Payment processing errors occur.
- A Job fails before compute resources are allocated.

Automatic refunds may be processed without requiring a support request.

---

# 8. Partial Refunds

Where appropriate, UniGPU may issue partial refunds based on verified usage.

Examples include:

- A Job that terminates prematurely due to Provider hardware failure.
- Platform outages affecting execution.
- Verified billing inaccuracies.
- Infrastructure failures during execution.

Refund amounts may reflect actual compute resources successfully consumed before interruption.

---

# 9. Non-Refundable Charges

Unless required by applicable law, the following are generally non-refundable:

- Successfully completed Compute Jobs.
- User-requested cancellation after substantial execution has occurred.
- Charges resulting from incorrectly configured Jobs.
- Charges caused by invalid code, datasets, or dependencies supplied by the Client.
- Charges resulting from unsupported software or incompatible workloads.
- Promotional credits.
- Expired promotional offers.

Refunds will not be issued solely because a Job produced unexpected or undesired results.

---

# 10. Billing Errors

If you believe your account has been billed incorrectly, you should notify UniGPU as soon as reasonably possible.

When reporting a billing issue, please include:

- Account email
- Job ID
- Transaction ID
- Date of transaction
- Description of the issue
- Supporting documentation, if available

UniGPU will investigate verified billing disputes in a commercially reasonable timeframe.

---

# 11. Chargebacks

Users should contact UniGPU before initiating a chargeback with their payment provider.

Initiating fraudulent or abusive chargebacks may result in:

- Temporary Account suspension
- Permanent Account termination
- Recovery of outstanding balances
- Restriction from future Services
- Legal action where appropriate

Nothing in this section limits a User's rights under applicable consumer protection laws.

---

# 12. Promotional Credits

From time to time, UniGPU may issue promotional credits.

Unless otherwise stated:

- Promotional credits have no cash value.
- Promotional credits are non-transferable.
- Promotional credits cannot be redeemed for cash.
- Promotional credits may expire according to the applicable promotion.
- UniGPU may revoke promotional credits obtained through fraud or abuse.

# 13. Taxes & Fees

Refunds generally exclude:

- Government taxes already remitted where non-refundable.
- Banking fees.
- Currency conversion fees.
- Third-party payment processing fees where applicable.

Applicable tax laws may affect the amount of any refund.
