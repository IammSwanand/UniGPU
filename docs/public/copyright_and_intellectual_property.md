# 7. Copyright Infringement

UniGPU respects valid intellectual property rights and may respond to notices alleging infringement.

Where appropriate, UniGPU may:

- Remove allegedly infringing content.
- Disable access to affected content.
- Suspend Compute Jobs.
- Restrict Accounts.
- Request additional information from the reporting party.
- Notify the affected User.
- Take other actions permitted by applicable law.

Submission of an infringement notice does not automatically result in removal of content.

UniGPU may investigate the circumstances before taking action.

# 8. Reporting Copyright Claims

If you believe that content available through the Services infringes your copyright or other intellectual property rights, please provide a written notice including, where applicable:

- Your name.
- Organization (if applicable).
- Contact information.
- Description of the copyrighted work.
- Description of the allegedly infringing content.
- Location of the content (such as Job ID or Account information).
- A statement that you have a good-faith belief that the use is unauthorized.
- A statement that the information provided is accurate and complete.
- Your electronic or physical signature, where required.

Incomplete reports may delay review.

---

# 9. Counter-Notifications

If your content is removed based on an infringement report and you believe the removal was made in error, you may submit a counter-notification.

Where appropriate, a counter-notification should include:

- Your contact information.
- Identification of the removed content.
- An explanation of why you believe the removal was incorrect.
- Supporting documentation where available.

UniGPU may review counter-notifications and, where appropriate, restore access to the content in accordance with applicable law.

# 10. Repeat Infringers

UniGPU reserves the right to suspend or permanently terminate Accounts that repeatedly infringe the intellectual property rights of others.

When determining whether an Account constitutes a repeat infringer, UniGPU may consider:

- Number of verified infringement notices.
- Severity of violations.
- Evidence submitted.
- Good-faith cooperation.
- Applicable legal requirements.
