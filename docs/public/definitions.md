# Legal Definitions

> **Version:** 1.0
> **Effective Date:** July 16, 2026
> **Last Updated:** July 16, 2026

---

## Purpose

These Legal Definitions apply to all UniGPU legal documents unless a document expressly defines a term differently.

The purpose of this document is to ensure consistent interpretation across the UniGPU platform and its policies.

---

# A

## Account

A registered UniGPU user profile that enables access to the Services.

Each Account is associated with a unique email address and is governed by the Terms of Service.

---

## API

The application programming interfaces made available by UniGPU that allow software to interact with the Services.

---

## API Key

A credential issued by UniGPU for authenticating API requests.

Users are responsible for keeping API Keys confidential.

---

## Applicable Law

Any law, regulation, governmental order, court order, sanction, or legally binding requirement applicable to UniGPU or the User.

---

# B

## Billing Cycle

The recurring period during which charges for Services are calculated.

---

# C

## Client

A User or Organization that consumes computing resources through the UniGPU platform.

Clients submit Jobs for execution.

---

## Compute Credits

Digital platform credits that may be used to purchase eligible UniGPU Services.

Compute Credits:

- are not legal tender,
- have no intrinsic monetary value,
- are non-transferable unless expressly permitted,
- may expire where permitted by applicable law.

---

## Compute Hour

A billable unit representing one hour of allocated compute resources.

Partial usage may be billed according to the applicable pricing model.

---

## Container

An isolated runtime environment used to execute Client workloads.

Containers are managed by UniGPU or the Provider Agent depending on the execution model.

---

# D

## Dashboard

The authenticated web interface used to access UniGPU Services.

---

## Documentation

Official technical documentation, guides, SDKs, tutorials, policies, and manuals published by UniGPU.

---

# E

## Enterprise Customer

A business, institution, or organization using UniGPU under a commercial agreement.

Enterprise Customers may be subject to additional contractual terms.

---

# G

## GPU Instance

A logical allocation of one or more GPUs made available to execute a Job.

---

# H

## Hardware Verification

The process through which UniGPU validates the identity and specifications of Provider hardware before it is listed on the marketplace.

Verification may include automated testing, benchmarking, software inspection, and other integrity checks.

---

# J

## Job

A compute workload submitted by a Client.

A Job may include, but is not limited to:

- source code
- machine learning models
- datasets
- scripts
- configuration files
- container images
- dependencies

---

## Job Output

Any files, models, logs, checkpoints, or other artifacts generated by executing a Job.

Ownership of Job Output remains with the Client unless otherwise agreed.

---

# M

## Marketplace

The UniGPU platform through which Clients discover and rent compute resources offered by Providers.

---

# O

## Organization

A company, university, laboratory, government entity, nonprofit organization, or other legal entity using UniGPU.

---

# P

## Platform

The complete UniGPU ecosystem including:

- Website
- Dashboard
- Marketplace
- APIs
- Provider Agent
- Client Software
- Billing Systems
- Enterprise Services
- Infrastructure

---

## Provider

A User or Organization that contributes eligible compute resources to the Marketplace.

Providers execute Client Jobs through the Provider Agent.

---

## Provider Agent

Software developed by UniGPU and installed on Provider hardware.

The Provider Agent communicates securely with the Platform and is responsible for:

- receiving Jobs,
- preparing execution environments,
- reporting hardware status,
- transmitting execution results,
- enforcing platform policies.

---

# S

## Services

Collectively, all products and services provided by UniGPU, including but not limited to:

- Website
- Dashboard
- Marketplace
- APIs
- Provider Agent
- Billing
- Enterprise Services
- Documentation

---

## Session

A temporary authenticated interaction between a User and the Platform.

---

# T

## Third-Party Service

A product or service operated by an entity other than UniGPU that integrates with the Platform.

Examples include:

- payment processors,
- authentication providers,
- email providers,
- cloud infrastructure providers,
- analytics providers.

---

# U

## User

Any individual or Organization that accesses or uses the Services.

---

## User Content

Any information uploaded, submitted, transmitted, or stored through UniGPU, including:

- source code,
- machine learning models,
- datasets,
- prompts,
- scripts,
- notebooks,
- configuration files,
- container images,
- documentation,
- other digital content.

Unless otherwise stated, Users retain ownership of their User Content.

---

# W

## Wallet

The balance of Compute Credits, promotional credits, refunds, and transaction history associated with a User Account.

---

## Workload

Any computational task executed through the Platform.

Examples include:

- AI training,
- AI inference,
- rendering,
- scientific computing,
- simulations,
- data processing,
- software compilation.

---

# Interpretation

Unless the context requires otherwise:

- Singular includes the plural.
- Plural includes the singular.
- References to "including" mean "including without limitation."
- Headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.
- References to one gender include all genders.
