# 1. Introduction

This Enterprise Service Level Agreement ("SLA") describes the service availability commitments provided by UniGPU for eligible Enterprise Customers.

This SLA supplements the Terms of Service and any applicable Enterprise Agreement.

Unless otherwise agreed in writing, this SLA applies only to Enterprise Services purchased directly from UniGPU.

---

# 2. Definitions

Capitalized terms used in this SLA have the meanings assigned to them in the **Legal Definitions** document unless expressly defined otherwise.

---

# 3. Scope

This SLA applies to eligible Enterprise Services including:

- Enterprise Dashboard
- Enterprise APIs
- Compute Scheduling
- Billing Services
- Authentication Services
- Organization Management
- Enterprise Support

Unless specifically stated otherwise, this SLA does not apply to Community or Free-tier services.

# 4. Service Availability

UniGPU targets a Monthly Uptime Percentage of:

| Service | Target Availability |
|----------|-------------------:|
| Authentication | 99.9% |
| Dashboard | 99.9% |
| APIs | 99.9% |
| Billing | 99.9% |
| Enterprise Portal | 99.9% |

These availability targets are objectives and are measured monthly.

---

# 5. Scheduled Maintenance

UniGPU may perform scheduled maintenance to improve reliability and security.

Where reasonably practicable:

- Customers will receive advance notice.
- Maintenance will be scheduled during low-traffic periods.
- Scheduled maintenance is excluded from uptime calculations.

Emergency maintenance may occur without prior notice where necessary to protect the Services.

---

# 6. Service Availability Calculation

Monthly Availability is calculated as:

Availability = ((Total Minutes - Downtime Minutes) ÷ Total Minutes) × 100

Downtime includes verified periods during which the covered Enterprise Service is unavailable to the Customer.

Downtime does not include any exclusions described in this SLA.

---

# 7. Service Credits

If Monthly Availability falls below the applicable target due to UniGPU's failure to meet this SLA, eligible Enterprise Customers may request service credits.

| Monthly Availability | Service Credit |
|----------------------|---------------:|
| 99.9% or higher | None |
| 99.0% – 99.89% | 5% of monthly service fees |
| 95.0% – 98.99% | 10% of monthly service fees |
| Below 95.0% | 20% of monthly service fees |

Service credits:

- Are applied to future invoices.
- Have no cash value.
- Are the Customer's sole remedy for SLA violations unless otherwise required by applicable law.

Requests for service credits must be submitted within thirty (30) days after the end of the affected billing month.

# 9. Incident Severity Levels

## Severity 1 — Critical

Examples:

- Complete platform outage
- Authentication unavailable
- Billing system unavailable
- Data loss affecting multiple customers

**Target Initial Response:** Within 1 hour

---

## Severity 2 — High

Examples:

- Major feature unavailable
- Significant performance degradation
- Partial API outage

**Target Initial Response:** Within 4 business hours

---

## Severity 3 — Medium

Examples:

- Minor feature malfunction
- Limited service degradation
- Non-critical defects

**Target Initial Response:** Within 1 business day

---

## Severity 4 — Low

Examples:

- Documentation issues
- General questions
- Cosmetic defects
- Feature requests

**Target Initial Response:** Within 3 business days

Response times are targets and are not guarantees of resolution.

# 10. Customer Responsibilities

Customers are responsible for:

- Maintaining valid account credentials.
- Following UniGPU documentation.
- Keeping integrations up to date.
- Promptly reporting incidents.
- Providing information reasonably necessary for troubleshooting.

Failure to cooperate may affect UniGPU's ability to provide support.

---

# 11. SLA Exclusions

This SLA does not apply to service interruptions resulting from:

- Scheduled maintenance.
- Emergency maintenance.
- Internet connectivity outside UniGPU's control.
- Customer software.
- Customer configuration errors.
- Unsupported integrations.
- Third-party service failures.
- Force majeure events.
- Distributed denial-of-service attacks.
- Customer misuse.
- Violations of UniGPU policies.

Provider-specific hardware failures affecting individual Marketplace resources do not automatically constitute an SLA violation unless they result in covered Enterprise Service downtime.
