# 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy ("Policy") explains the standards governing the use of the UniGPU platform.

Its purpose is to protect the security, reliability, availability, and integrity of the Services, the Marketplace, Providers, Clients, and third parties.

Every User is required to comply with this Policy while using the Services.

Violation of this Policy may result in warnings, suspension, permanent account termination, removal of content, cancellation of Jobs, withholding of payouts where permitted by law, or other actions deemed necessary to protect the platform.

---

# 2. Definitions

Capitalized terms used in this Policy have the meanings assigned to them in the **Legal Definitions** document unless expressly defined otherwise.

---

# 3. Scope

This Policy applies to every User of the Services, including but not limited to:

- Website
- Dashboard
- APIs
- Marketplace
- Provider Agent
- Enterprise Services
- Compute Jobs
- Wallet
- Billing Services

This Policy applies regardless of whether the User is a Client, Provider, Organization, or Enterprise Customer.

---

# 4. General Responsibilities

Users agree to:

- Use the Services lawfully.
- Respect the rights of other Users.
- Protect account credentials.
- Provide accurate information.
- Follow applicable laws and regulations.
- Cooperate with legitimate security investigations conducted by UniGPU.

Users are responsible for all activity conducted through their Accounts.

---

# 5. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are prohibited.

---

## 5.1 Illegal Activity

Users may not use the Services to:

- Commit criminal offenses.
- Facilitate criminal conduct.
- Launder money.
- Evade sanctions.
- Violate export-control laws.
- Distribute illegal material.
- Operate unlawful marketplaces.

---

## 5.2 Fraud

Users may not:

- Misrepresent their identity.
- Submit false hardware specifications.
- Manipulate billing systems.
- Abuse referral programs.
- Circumvent payment systems.
- Create fraudulent Accounts.
- Engage in deceptive commercial practices.

---

## 5.3 Unauthorized Access

Users may not attempt to:

- Gain unauthorized access to Accounts.
- Access Provider systems without authorization.
- Bypass authentication mechanisms.
- Escalate privileges.
- Exploit vulnerabilities without authorization.
- Access data belonging to another User.

---

## 5.4 Credential Theft

Users may not:

- Harvest passwords.
- Collect authentication tokens.
- Conduct phishing campaigns.
- Deploy credential stealers.
- Perform credential stuffing attacks.
- Capture authentication secrets.

---

## 5.5 Privacy Violations

Users may not:

- Access another User's data without authorization.
- Collect personal information unlawfully.
- Monitor communications without consent.
- Intercept network traffic belonging to others.

---

# 6. Security Violations

The following activities are prohibited:

- Port scanning systems you do not own.
- Denial-of-Service (DoS) attacks.
- Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attacks.
- Packet interception.
- Traffic manipulation.
- Network disruption.
- Unauthorized penetration testing.
- Security exploitation of UniGPU infrastructure.
- Attempts to disable platform security controls.

Legitimate security research is permitted only with prior written authorization from UniGPU.

---

# 7. Malware & Harmful Software

Users may not develop, distribute, execute, or host:

- Malware
- Ransomware
- Spyware
- Trojan horses
- Worms
- Rootkits
- Botnets
- Remote Access Trojans (RATs)
- Credential stealers
- Cryptojacking software
- Malicious payloads
- Exploit kits

Any workload determined to intentionally distribute or facilitate malicious software may be terminated immediately.

---

# 8. Platform Abuse

Users may not:

- Abuse free-tier resources.
- Circumvent platform rate limits.
- Artificially manipulate benchmark results.
- Manipulate Provider reputation.
- Manipulate Client ratings.
- Bypass scheduling systems.
- Bypass billing mechanisms.
- Interfere with Marketplace operations.
- Generate excessive artificial workloads intended to degrade platform performance.

---

# 9. Intellectual Property

Users may not knowingly upload or execute workloads that infringe the intellectual property rights of others.

Examples include:

- Pirated software.
- Copyright-infringing datasets.
- Unauthorized machine learning models.
- Proprietary source code without authorization.
- Counterfeit digital assets.

Users remain solely responsible for ensuring they possess all necessary rights to their User Content.

---

# 10. AI & Machine Learning Workloads

UniGPU supports artificial intelligence, machine learning, scientific computing, rendering, and other legitimate compute workloads.

However, Users may not knowingly use the Services to:

- Generate illegal content.
- Produce malicious software.
- Automate cyberattacks.
- Conduct unauthorized surveillance.
- Circumvent applicable law.
- Facilitate identity theft.
- Create systems primarily intended for criminal activity.

UniGPU does not routinely inspect User Content but reserves the right to investigate credible reports of abuse.

---

# 11. Cryptocurrency Mining

Unless expressly authorized by UniGPU through a separate product, commercial agreement, or service offering, Users may not use the Services for:

- Proof-of-work cryptocurrency mining.
- Blockchain validation.
- Cryptojacking.
- Unauthorized mining pools.
- Workloads primarily intended to generate cryptocurrency.

If UniGPU introduces mining-specific products in the future, those products may be governed by separate terms.

---

# 12. Provider Responsibilities

In addition to all other obligations, Providers agree that they will not:

- Inspect Client User Content without authorization.
- Copy Client files.
- Modify Client workloads.
- Alter execution results.
- Misrepresent hardware specifications.
- Falsify benchmark data.
- Interfere with billing.
- Intentionally disconnect systems during active Jobs.
- Attempt to recover Client data after Job completion.
- Install software intended to compromise Client workloads.

Failure to comply may result in immediate removal from the Marketplace.

---

# 13. Enforcement

UniGPU may investigate suspected violations of this Policy.

Where appropriate, UniGPU may:

- Issue warnings.
- Suspend Jobs.
- Restrict Marketplace access.
- Remove uploaded content.
- Freeze payouts where permitted by law.
- Suspend Accounts.
- Permanently terminate Accounts.
- Report unlawful activity to competent authorities.
- Cooperate with lawful investigations.

UniGPU reserves the right to take immediate action where necessary to protect the Services, Users, Providers, or third parties.

---

# 14. Reporting Abuse

If you believe this Policy has been violated, please report the incident.

Where possible, include:

- Username
- Job ID
- Provider ID (if applicable)
- Date and time
- Description of the incident
- Supporting evidence

Reports should be submitted to:

**Abuse**

abuse@unigpu.in

**Security**

security@unigpu.in
